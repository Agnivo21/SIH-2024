Constituent Assembly Debates

VIEW ALL →

The Constituent Assembly of India was formed in 1946 to draft the Constitution. It was a mix of elected and nominated members. The Assembly first met on December 9, 1946,chaired by Dr. Rajendra Prasad, with Dr. B.R. Ambedkar heading the Drafting Committee. After 11 sessions over nearly three years, the Constitution was adopted on November 26, 1949, and came into effect on January 26, 1950.

Historical Constitutions

VIEW ALL →

The Constitution of India is the supreme law, outlining political principles, procedures, and government powers. Adopted on November 26, 1949, and effective from January 26, 1950, it replaced the Government of India Act 1935, transitioning India from a British dominion to a sovereign republic. It is the longest written constitution, drawing from various sources, including the Government of India Acts and the Indian Independence Act 1947.

Committee Reports

VIEW ALL →

The drafting of the Indian Constitution involved several key committee reports, including: Draft Constitution by the Constitutional Adviser (Oct 1, 1947) Ad Hoc Committee on Citizenship Clause (May 1, 1947) Union Constitution Committee (July 4, 1947) Advisory Committee on Political Safeguards for Minorities (May 11, 1949) Sub-Committee on Fundamental Rights (April 16, 1947). These reports provided crucial recommendations and frameworks for the Constitution.

Discover the journey of India’s Constitution:
Constituent Assembly: Formed in 1946 with 389 members, later 299 after partition.
Drafting Committee: Led by Dr. B.R. Ambedkar, included top leaders and experts.
Debates: 11 sessions over 165 days to shape the final document.
Adoption: Adopted on Nov 26, 1949, effective from Jan 26, 1950 (Republic Day).
Influences:Inspired by British, American, Irish, and French constitutions.
